My journey with photography began in 2017, but my fascination with images started even earlier through astronomy. I spent years learning and practising how to observe the night sky, which eventually led me to buy my first DSLR to photograph the Moon and deep-space objects. That experience shaped the way I see light, patience, and detail—things that still influence my work today. Over time, my focus shifted from the stars to life on Earth. I found myself drawn to artistic photography, especially to capturing the essence of a moment or a person. I love photographing people and creating impactful portraits that feel honest and emotional, but I’m equally inspired by dramatic, beautiful landscapes. My main style is black and white, and I’m deeply passionate about analogue photography. Working with film negatives slows the process down and makes every frame intentional—something I believe adds depth and authenticity to the final image. Through my photographs, I try to show the world differently—through the way I see it, sense it, and feel it. My work often reflects my character and emotional state, turning each image into a personal expression rather than just a visual record.
